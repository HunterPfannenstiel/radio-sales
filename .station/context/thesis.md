# Thesis

Progress synthesis drives adoption - "If this app will tell me exactly what I need to do to reach my goals then I will use it"

## Rep's Number One Question

"Where do I currently stand in relation to my goals?"

### Goal Relationship

A rep needs to understand where they stand in relation to their goals. That is the number one priority. Any data that doesn't support this is not top priority.

### Core Functionality

Every core piece of functionality needs to pass the test of "Will this help the rep know where they stand in relation to their goals?". If a piece of functionality doesn't answer yes then it is not core to the application. This doesn't mean the functionality doesn't serve a place, but it should not be presented in such a way that demands the user's attention.

#### Relative Over Absolute

Surfacing "5 more calls this week to stay on pace" is better than the rep having to analyze that they made 15 calls and their goal is 20. We should present directly to the rep what they want to know rather than making them figure it out.

Relative over absolute. Relative information has higher precedence over absolute information.

Absolute data still has a place but it plays the role of reflection and adjustment.

### Incentivise Through Surfacing

The main behavior reinforcer will be real time updates to the rep's progress towards their goals

The feedback loop is such that if I know that when I input this data it will curate a more accurate version of where I stand in relation to my goals, then inputting data is super benefical and I never want to skip it.
