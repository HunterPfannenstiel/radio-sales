# How Reps Use the App

## The Core Behavior: High Intent, Not Browsing

Radio sales reps are not browsers. They come in with a specific job every time. The session has intent before they open the app. Every screen should be optimized for a rep who arrived with a purpose and wants to leave in under a minute.

If a rep is looking at their business list with no specific business in mind, the app has failed them — it should have surfaced the right business before they had to go looking.

## The Three Sessions

**1. Post-call, in the car**
They just walked out of a meeting. They're sitting in the parking lot. They open the app to log what happened before they forget. They're not checking their dashboard, not looking at other businesses — one job, then they're closing the app and driving to the next appointment. Highest frequency session.

**2. Pre-call prep, also in the car**
Parked outside a business with 20 minutes to spare. They pull up that business to remember where they left off, what's pending, what they committed to. One job, get in, get out.

**3. Morning check-in**
Monday or start of day. They look at their pace — "where am I, what do I need this week?" The closest thing to browsing, but still purposeful. Calibrating the week, not exploring.

## The Plan Already Exists Before They Open the App

A broadcast media rep plans their week on Monday morning — who they're calling on, roughly what they're selling, what their weekly target is. That planning happens in their head, in their calendar, in conversation with their manager. The app doesn't help them plan. **The app keeps the plan honest.**

It captures that the plan is being executed. It surfaces when the plan has gaps the rep didn't notice. It tells them if their activity matches their goal or if they're falling behind.

The relationship: plan lives in the rep's head → execution happens in the field → app is the capture layer and the reality check.

## The Demo Implication

Don't demo this app like a dashboard you sit and explore. Demo it as three 30-second sessions with a specific job each time. That framing itself is the pitch — this app respects a rep's time in a way nothing else does.
