# Actions as Primary — Rep Experience Philosophy

## The Core Idea

Flip the hierarchy. Instead of a dashboard with numbers first and actionable items as secondary context, make the synthesized action the primary thing a rep sees. Numbers become supporting context for the rep who wants to understand why.

The action item — "make 3 calls today, ask for $7,667 this week" — is already the synthesis of goal + current progress + time remaining, calculated. The rep doesn't have to do any math. They just act.

## The Distinction

A dashboard is a scoreboard. It tells you where you are.

A rep at 2pm on Wednesday doesn't need a scoreboard — they need a coach. Those are different things.

The numbers below the action answer "why" for the rep who wants to understand. But most reps most of the time want to know what to do next.

## The Weight Loss App Parallel

MyFitnessPal shows you your calorie count. The best fitness apps tell you to go for a walk. The action IS the synthesis — it just hides the math. Same idea here.

## The Critical Requirement: It Must Be Dynamic

The action has to update in real time based on actual progress or it becomes wallpaper. If it always says "make 5 calls today" regardless of whether you've already made 3, reps stop reading it within a week.

The moment it says "you've hit today's call target" when you actually have — that's the moment it feels like it knows you. That responsiveness is what makes it coaching instead of just a static label.

## State-Based Coaching (Future)

The same surface can have different emotional registers based on the rep's state:

- **Behind pace** — directive: "you need 3 more calls today"
- **On pace** — affirming: "you're on track, keep the pace"
- **Ahead** — celebratory: "you've cleared today's target, here's what got you here"

This is the same information presented differently based on context. It's coaching, not just reporting.

## Why This Is Differentiated

No CRM on the market does this. CRMs are all scoreboards — they tell you what happened. This tells you what to do right now, derived from your goal. That's the competitive angle.

## What Currently Exists

The app shows "Present $7,667/wk to hit your goal" — a weekly money number. This is correct framing (asks accumulate weekly, not daily). The gap is the daily behavior lever: calls. A rep can make a call any moment. They can't always force an ask (asks require the right sales stage). So the daily call count is the only number that earns a place alongside the weekly ask target.

## The Proposed Structure

**Primary (above the fold):**
- Synthesized action derived from goal + progress + time remaining
- Dynamically updated as reps log activity

**Secondary (accessible, not competing):**
- The actual numbers: Sold, Projected, % to goal, calls logged, asks logged
- For the rep who wants to understand the math behind the action
