# Rep Experience Ideas

These are differentiation ideas for the rep side — things that go beyond what's in the feature docs and answer the thesis: goals are the north star, minimal tension between intent and execution.

The test for every idea: does it answer "what does this mean for my goal?" If not, it doesn't belong here.

---

## The "What Do I Need Today" Number

Not a chart, not a dashboard — one sentence. "You need 3 more calls today to stay on pace." Derived from weekly target, days remaining in the week, and calls logged so far. CRMs track history. This tells you what to do right now.

---

## Streak + Personal Bests

The rep who hits their call target 10 days straight should feel that. A private streak that becomes something you don't want to break — think Duolingo. Personal bests for calls in a week, asks in a week, dollars asked in a week. Competitive with yourself, never exposed to others.

---

## Dead Deals Resurfacing

No notification. But on the dashboard, quietly visible: "4 deals haven't moved in 7 days." Pending money sitting stale, surfaced passively. Keeps the rep honest without building a task manager or becoming a CRM.

---

## The Ask Gap

One specific number on the dashboard: "To hit your goal this month, you need to ask for $X more this week." Derived from the monthly goal gap plus the rep's historical close rate. Makes the abstract monthly goal feel daily and actionable.

---

## The "Ask Up" Whisper

When a rep logs an ask below their average account value, a subtle note — not a block, not an alert. Just: "This ask is below your average." Plants the seed without being preachy. Over time, average account value climbs because reps are thinking about it at the moment of entry.

---

## Monthly Reset Ritual

First of the month: a clean summary of last month (calls, asks, dollars asked, dollars sold), then the new goal front and center. Not a notification — just the experience of the app feeling like a new season starting. A coach, not a database.
