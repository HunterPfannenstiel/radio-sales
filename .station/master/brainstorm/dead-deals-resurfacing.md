# Dead Deals Resurfacing

## The Core Idea

Pending deals that have passed their self-set "what's next" date surface passively on the rep dashboard. Not a notification, not a task manager — just visible context that says "this money is sitting still."

## The "What's Next" Connection

This idea only works because the quick log already captures a structured "what's next" selection. That field isn't just data — it's a clock the rep sets on themselves. "Follow up in 7 days" means the rep made a commitment. If 7 days pass with no new log, the deal is dead by their own definition.

So "dead" means: **your self-set next step date has passed and you haven't logged anything.** The rep owns it. The app just makes it visible. This is more precise than a blanket "no activity in X days" threshold, and it makes the "what's next" selection feel meaningful at logging time — not just a field you fill in.

## Two Jobs, Two Surfaces

The summary card and the drill-down list are doing completely different things.

**The summary card** (on the dashboard) is the coaching statement. It quantifies the problem without requiring the rep to think. They don't have to mentally audit their pipeline — the app already did it.

> *"4 deals need attention — $47,000 pending"*

The dollar amount is the key detail. "4 deals need attention" is fine. "$47,000 pending" is the moment a rep stops and pays attention. Connect it to the goal gap and it becomes a coaching insight, not a task list item.

**The drill-down list** (tap to enter) is the examination. The rep can triage in 10 seconds: which deal is biggest, which has been sitting longest, what did I commit to. That's the information a manager would surface in a coaching conversation — the app is just doing it first.

## The Drill-Down List

Four data points per row, nothing more:

- Business name
- What the rep committed to ("send proposal", "follow up in 7 days")
- The ask amount
- How overdue it is

How overdue is doing a lot of work. "3 days overdue" and "21 days overdue" are completely different urgency levels. Order the list by a combination of days overdue and ask size — the rep gets their priority order for the afternoon without having to think about it.

## The Flow

Tap a deal → land in that business with the last interaction visible and a prominent "log new call" at the top. The rep sees exactly what they committed to, logs the follow-up, and the deal disappears from the dead list in real time.

That disappearance is the payoff. The rep didn't pull up a CRM, didn't search for an account, didn't reconstruct context. They tapped, remembered, acted, done.

## Why This Works for Reps

The biggest failure mode in sales isn't forgetting accounts exist — reps know who their accounts are. It's forgetting what they committed to and when. This surfaces exactly that, with the dollar amount attached so it doesn't feel like admin — it feels like money.

## Demo Flow

Requires a pre-loaded demo account with deals that have overdue next steps. The walkthrough:

1. Rep opens the dashboard — sees actions as primary, then below: *"4 deals need attention — $47,000 pending"*
2. Tap → clean list of businesses, each showing the overdue commitment, ask amount, and days overdue — ordered by urgency
3. Tap one → lands in that business with last interaction visible, "log new call" prominent at top
4. Rep logs a new interaction, sets a new "what's next"
5. Deal disappears from the list in real time

> "The rep didn't check a pipeline report. They didn't get a nag from their manager. The app surfaced the money closest to closing and pointed them back at it. That's the difference between a scoreboard and a coach."
