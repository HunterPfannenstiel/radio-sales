I want to create a web-based, mobile-first software product for broadcast media sales professionals that keeps them focused on the daily sales activity that actually leads to hitting their sales goals. The core idea is seller-first, not customer-first. I don’t want this to become a full CRM or a complicated reporting suite. I want it to be a simple, motivating system that helps reps stay efficient and productive, and helps managers coach performance using clear activity and results data.

For the sales rep, the most important thing the app should answer every day is: how many sales calls do I need to make today, and how much do I need to ask for today and this week? A “call” in this system is a real sales call (in person, by phone, or a virtual meeting). The rep should be able to quickly log a call by opening a browser on their phone, typing a business name (business name only for v1), selecting where they are in the sales process, briefly describing the idea, entering how much ad budget they asked for, entering the term of the schedule, selecting their confidence level, and marking the outcome as pending, yes, or no. The sales process stages I want to track are approach, uncover, present, close, and service/upsell. A key part of the system is tracking “what’s next” so every opportunity keeps progressing, with next-step options like scheduling the next meeting, sending a proposal, booking a close meeting, or following up in a set number of days.

The app should feel motivating and challenging, like a weight loss app that drives good daily habits. I prefer private streaks and personal bests for reps. I also like the idea of a staff leaderboard, but without exposing dollar amounts—just a percent-to-goal leaderboard to drive healthy competitiveness. The leaderboard should be based on Sold percent to goal (results), not Projected.

The main dashboard concept is an airplane “flight deck” with gauges. The one main gauge is money pace—whether the rep is gaining or losing altitude toward their sales goal. Activity pace supports that main number, so reps can see both money pace and activity pace at a glance. The system should track both Sold and Projected. Sold is what is “in.” Projected is a weighted forecast based on the rep’s confidence in the sale at the time they log the ask. I want the rep to choose how they feel about the sale using four confidence levels: IN, SURE, EXPECT, and HOPE. The weights are fixed for v1 and not configurable: IN dollars at 95%, SURE dollars at 80%, EXPECT dollars at 40%, and HOPE dollars at 10%. The dashboard should show Sold, Projected, goal, percent to goal, and the gap needed to reach the goal.

The system should also track activity standards set by the sales manager for each rep. A default example is 25 sales calls per week and 5 asks per week, but the manager sets each rep’s targets and goals in consultation with the rep. We assume a Monday through Friday work week and track the year by week number. The rep’s activity is self-driven (no manager-assigned tasks inside the app). The accountability is the shared visibility between the rep and the sales manager. For v1, I want it to be purely “check the dashboard” with no proactive notifications.

Managers need a view that is like Google Analytics for sales activity—high-level performance plus the ability to drill into details when someone is behind pace. When a rep is behind, the manager should be able to click in and see whether the rep’s activity is poor, whether they are making unfocused calls that don’t move deals forward, whether their qualifying/prospecting is weak, or whether they aren’t asking for enough dollars. The system should calculate key performance indicators automatically, including closing ratio (based on asks vs sold) and average account value.

Because some marketing plans are one-month schedules and others are longer-term (including annual buys), the system needs to capture schedule length so it can calculate a monthly equivalent value. For example, a $15,000 annual buy should translate to $1,250 per month. Reps should enter term length as a number plus a selection of weeks or months, and the system standardizes reporting to months using a simple conversion of 4 weeks per month. This monthly equivalent is important for calculating average account value and for flagging low asks. “Not asking for enough dollars” can mean the rep’s asks are below the current average monthly account value (for example, $540/month), and one goal is to raise that average over time.

At the executive level (CEO/COO/CFO), the view is about overall performance by property and product. Executives should be able to see goal and percent to goal for each property (station, market/cluster) and for product lines (like digital sales). They should have both a Sold view (as percent to goal) and a Projected view for forecasting. The rollups should flow from rep to manager/station to corporate, with drill-down available when needed.

For rollout success, the first thing I want to see is adoption and consistent use—reps logging focused calls and asks, and managers using the dashboards for coaching and accountability. Sold and Projected improvements are the result of good activity and focus over time. For v1, I do not want to integrate with any other systems; I want this to stand alone and prove value through daily usage and better sales discipline.
