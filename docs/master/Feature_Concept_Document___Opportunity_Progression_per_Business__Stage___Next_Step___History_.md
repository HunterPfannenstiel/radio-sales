A rep needs a simple way to keep each business moving forward through the sales process without turning the product into a full CRM. This feature defines how a business is represented over time, how its current status is tracked, and how the rep uses that status to drive self-directed daily execution. The goal is to make it easy for a rep to see what’s next across their accounts and to keep opportunities progressing through approach, uncover, present, close, and service/upsell.

Each business has a current stage and a current next step. These represent the rep’s current view of where that business sits in the process and what the next action should be. The current stage and current next step can be updated over time based on the rep’s work, and the system always treats the currently saved values as the business’s current status.

A rep can update a business’s current stage and current next step in two ways. First, when they log a new interaction, the business’s current stage and next step can be set as part of that log. Second, the rep can directly edit the business’s current stage and current next step without logging a new interaction. This direct edit is intended as lightweight housekeeping to keep the rep’s pipeline organized and accurate without forcing extra logging. When a rep edits the current stage or next step directly, the system simply updates the current status and does not create a change history record.

The system maintains a history of interactions for each business as a separate concept from the editable current status. Reps can view the list of logged interactions over time for a business, including the stages and any optional ask information captured in those logs. This history supports coaching and review, while the editable current status supports day-to-day execution.

Reps have a personal “what’s next” list view that is driven by the current next step values across their businesses. This is a self-directed execution tool that helps the rep prioritize productive work and stay focused. In v1, this list is based on the current next step value rather than a due-date or task scheduling system.

Managers do not have a team-wide rollup list of next steps across all reps. Instead, managers see next steps when they drill into a specific rep and view that rep’s businesses and their current status. This keeps the manager experience focused on performance dashboards and coaching drill-downs rather than task management.
