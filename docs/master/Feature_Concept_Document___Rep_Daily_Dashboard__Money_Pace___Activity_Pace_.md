A rep needs a simple, motivating place to check every day that answers the core question: what do I need to do today and this week to stay on pace for my sales goal? This feature is the rep’s daily dashboard. It is designed to keep the rep focused on efficient, productive sales activity first, and to make it obvious whether they are gaining or losing altitude toward their goal. The dashboard is check-the-dashboard only; it does not send notifications.

When a rep opens the dashboard, it defaults to the current week, paced across Monday through Friday. The dashboard shows both money pace and activity pace together so the rep can see the outcome and the behaviors that support it in one place. Money pace is the primary focus. It is anchored to the rep’s monthly goal and shows month-to-date Sold percent to goal as the main scoreboard, along with a single Projected total for coaching and forecasting. Projected is not broken out by confidence bucket on the rep dashboard; it is shown as one number that represents the weighted projection based on the rep’s confidence selections when they logged asks.

Activity pace supports the money gauge by showing the rep’s progress toward their weekly activity targets, specifically calls per week and asks per week. Weekly pacing assumes a Monday–Friday work week. If a rep logs activity outside of those days, it still counts toward the week’s totals, but the pacing expectations are based on Monday through Friday.

The dashboard has clear empty-state behavior. If it is the start of a month, or a rep is new, and there is no logged activity or sales yet, the dashboard still shows the rep’s targets and pace-to-date for the week, and it shows Sold and Projected as 0% to goal. This reinforces the habit loop immediately rather than hiding the scoreboard until data exists.

The dashboard supports historical review. A rep can switch to prior weeks and prior months to see past performance. Weekly views are navigated by week number, and monthly views are navigated by month. The dashboard reflects the rep’s current manager-set goals and targets, and if a manager changes a goal or target, the dashboard updates immediately and recalculates percent-to-goal and pacing based on the current values.
