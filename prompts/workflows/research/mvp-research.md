# Competitor Analysis Research Plan

### Smart Research Guidelines

1. **Mine Reviews with "Extreme Semantic Search"**
   * Use targeted keywords like *"wish it had"*, *"workaround"*, *"frustrated"*, and *"clunky"* on G2, Capterra, and Reddit.
   * Focus strictly on reviews from the **last 6 months** to capture the competitor's current product state.
2. **Focus on "Different, Not Better"**
   * Avoid trying to match established competitors feature-for-feature.
   * Identify steps in their workflow that your MVP can solve in fewer clicks or skip entirely.
3. **Disregard the "Loud Minority"**
   * Filter out hyper-specific edge-case complaints and one-off user rants.
   * Only log a complaint or feature gap if you see it repeated by **at least 3 different users**.
4. **Research without any bias**
    * Don't knit-pick specific things that validate your solution while omitting things that go against it.
    * Be brutally honest in your findings as biased research will ruin the product from the start.

---

### What to Research

#### 1. Objective & Scope Definition
* Identify 2 direct competitors and 1 indirect competitor or manual workaround.
* Define the MVP boundary line to explicitly establish what *not* to build.
* Choose the exact user action that proves your MVP successfully solved the core problem.

#### 2. Workflow & Feature Mapping
* Map the exact steps a user takes to complete the primary task in competing products.
* Document the mandatory features required to make that core workflow function.
* Note how quickly competitors get a new user from registration to their first value moment.

#### 3. Sentiment & Review Analysis
* Identify highly praised features to establish your absolute "must-have" product baseline.
* Log recurring bugs, missing features, and complex workflows from review platforms.
* Pinpoint low-hanging fruit—frequent complaints that are technically simple for your MVP to fix.

#### 4. Value Proposition & Positioning
* Analyze competitor landing pages to find the exact core promise they lead with.
* Draft a value proposition that directly answers the competitor's biggest market complaint.
* Set an MVP pricing strategy based on a lower price, flat fee, or high-value narrow niche.

#### 5. Pitfalls to Avoid
* Identify bloated, secondary features built by competitors that you must exclude from launch.
* Look for complex automations built by competitors that your MVP can safely handle manually at first.
* Spot areas where competitors struggle to update their product due to rigid underlying structures.
